Joe Rosenberg, PhD, Founder and President of V.I.P. Tours of New York, Architecture and Theatre Historian; lecturer, and NYC licensed tour guide.

I received a PhD in Medical Biochemistry from the Fels Cancer Research Institute. (I know, you didn’t see that coming!). It was my good fortune that a post-doctoral research fellowship, at the Albert Einstein College of Medicine, brought me to New York. It was love at first sight. The city enabled and encouraged me to change my life focus.

While I was developing my passion for architecture and theater, I was watching many historic buildings being torn down. I decided I had to do something about it, so I became part of a movement to save NY from losing its history. I re-activated the languishing New York Historic Districts Council, became its President, and began working closely with New York City’s Landmark Preservation Commission, pushing for the NYC landmark designation of 10 NYC Historic Districts including SoHo, DUMBO, Park Slope, and Fort Greene. (New York now has approximately 110 historic districts). I was a co-founder of “The Showpeople’s Committee to Save Radio City Music Hall”, in response to plans to demolish what was long-called the “Showplace of the Nation.” I co-founded, with Actor’s Equity, “Save The Theatres, Inc.” which fought for the landmark designations of 35 Broadway Theatres. I worked alongside Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis in the fierce battle to save Grand Central Station, a fight which went all the way to the U.S. Supreme Court, helping to shape the architectural preservation movement throughout the entire country.

Saving these historic buildings and districts ultimately became an asset to the city, which began to see new rebirth in the 90’s, in large part due to the efforts of the city to keep the arts and the artists in New York. The city became safe and people went back to the theatre. Fortunately, there were some theatres remaining to go back to. Today there are more upcoming shows than theatres available. This brought me to understand I needed to pass on our experiences. I started lecturing about New York Theatre and Motion Picture palaces nationally and internationally.

I became a Board member of the League of Historic American Theatres, dedicated to the restoration and re-use of historic theatres throughout the U.S. and Canada. I realized that the best way to show the achievements of NYC in the support of the arts and landmark preservation was by bringing people to NY in organized tours. I soon discovered that many regional and historic theatres and other performing arts organizations were very interested in planning trips for their board members, patrons, and financial supporters. To my delight, our programs were very effective in helping these organizations build bonds with their financial supporters, which resulted in increased donations and emotional engagement. We used this model to grow and expand our portfolio with other Cities including London, Edinburgh, San Francisco, Chicago and Havana.
