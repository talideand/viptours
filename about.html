About us

In the 70’s when New York was going through one of its worst economic and social crises, 50% of the theaters were dark at any given time. People thought we were close to the demise of Broadway. New York was losing many historic buildings, including theaters, train stations, and the hearts of several historic districts. Joe Rosenberg, V.I.P. Tours of New York founder, was one of the champions in the movement to save our historic buildings. Thanks to folks like him, we can enjoy today Radio City Music Hall, Grand Central Terminal, 41 Broadway theaters and many others. The renaissance of New York shows the importance of the arts and architectural preservation to the economic development of a city.

The focus of our company is sharing with our guests the stories of how New York was saved from losing its soul, while guiding our visitors to a deeper immersion in the fascinating world of the performing arts and landmark preservation.
